Race Week Preview: Miami Grand Prix

Rob Beaumont
Official F1 Fantasy columnist · formula1.com · Apr 29, 2026

> Three races in, Mercedes have won all of them. Here is your F1 Fantasy watchlist for Miami.

---

THE SETUP

Three races. Three wins for Mercedes. Russell took Australia, Antonelli took China and Japan back to back, and the Constructors' gap is already 45 points over Ferrari. Leclerc has podiumed in two of three rounds and Hamilton delivered a surprise China third — Ferrari are the only consistent second force. McLaren showed improvement at Suzuka with Piastri's second place, but here is the news that changes everything: Andrea Stella has hinted at a "brand new" McLaren arriving in Miami. Norris has won this race twice. Piastri won it in 2025. The five-week break is over and the field is closing in.

---

THE CIRCUIT

Miami is a 19-turn, street-adjacent layout where the long back straight into Turn 17 is the primary overtaking zone — moves are on, but clean air remains a significant advantage. Tyre degradation is high in the May heat, with track temperatures regularly exceeding 30°C and a two-stop strategy likely for most of the field. Safety car probability is elevated — tight walls and first-lap incidents are Miami traditions. Crucially, this is a Sprint weekend, meaning Sprint Qualifying, the Sprint, and the Race all score Fantasy points. Every driver and constructor in your team gets three sessions to earn.

---

THE WEATHER

Hot, humid and unstable. Miami in early May brings daily highs of 84–86°F and a meaningful chance of afternoon thunderstorms, particularly on race day. If rain arrives during Sprint Qualifying on Friday, grid positions become unpredictable and the positions-gained calculation changes entirely for Sunday. Watch the midweek forecast closely — confirmed race-day rain changes your chip call.

---

THE WEEKLY WATCHLIST

🟢 BUY

Oscar Piastri — ~$22m
McLaren are bringing their biggest upgrade package of the season to Miami. Stella hinted at a "brand new" car. Piastri finished second in Japan, the team has won here two years running, and the odds are shortening heading into the weekend. This is the race to back the upgrade arriving. If it does, he will be out of reach price-wise by next round.

Oliver Bearman — $7.4m
The best PPM on the grid after three rounds. Haas are legitimately competitive under the 2026 regulations and Bearman is the driver extracting every available point. Miami's characteristics — tight walls, safety car likelihood, first-lap chaos — favour point-scoring opportunism from a midfield car. He is the budget pick that makes everything else affordable.

---

🟡 HOLD

Kimi Antonelli — $23.2m
Two consecutive wins and the championship lead at 19 years old. Hold him. But if you do not own him already, the buy argument is complicated — his ownership has exploded and the McLaren upgrade adds genuine uncertainty at the front. He will score. Whether he tops the leaderboard this weekend is the question.

George Russell — $27.4m
Won Australia, backed Antonelli up in China. Miami's power-sensitive layout suits the fastest car on the grid. He is expensive relative to his teammate and Antonelli has clearly taken the lead role, but he is still finishing on the podium most weeks. Hold, do not panic sell.

---

🔴 SELL

Max Verstappen — $27.7m
He called the Red Bull undriveable in China and nothing has changed that picture. Red Bull have scored just 16 constructor points across three races. His price reflects the Verstappen of previous seasons — his 2026 points total absolutely does not. Miami's heat and grip demands will not flatter a car still searching for answers. Most expensive driver on the grid. Worst PPM in the top ten. Move on.

Lewis Hamilton — ~$20m
The China podium was exceptional and his price has moved to reflect it. Miami is a different circuit — qualifying pace matters and Leclerc will get ahead of him. Hamilton's realistic ceiling here is a solid points finish; his current valuation is pricing in another podium. If you are carrying him off the China result, this is your window to rotate before the price corrects.

---

THE CHIPS

Sprint weekends are the strongest use case for the 3X Boost chip — three scoring sessions, each one amplified by your captain multiplier. If your squad is set and the weather looks dry by Wednesday, this is a compelling round to play it. However, Miami's storm risk flips the calculation: confirmed rain on race day makes No Negative the smarter call. Check the forecast midweek and commit then. If neither chip fits your squad, the Wildcard is your third option — a five-week break with a clear read on the field is exactly the moment to reset.
