# Race Week Preview: Miami Grand Prix

**Rob Beaumont** — Official F1 Fantasy columnist · formula1.com · 22 April 2026

*Antonelli leads. Mercedes are running away with it. Miami needs to stop them.*

---

## THE SETUP

Three races in, the 2026 season already has its narrative: Mercedes are dominant, Kimi Antonelli is on fire, and everyone else is in damage limitation mode. The 19-year-old leads the championship by nine points from teammate George Russell after winning Japan via a perfectly-timed safety car restart. Ferrari are best of the rest — 90 constructors' points to Mercedes' 135 — with McLaren showing flashes but sitting 79 points back. Red Bull's 16-point tally after three rounds tells you everything you need to know about where the grid really is.

---

## THE CIRCUIT

The Miami International Autodrome doesn't reward pole sitters — it punishes them. In four editions of this race, not one pole-sitter has won. That's not a coincidence; it's a circuit profile. Three DRS zones and Miami heat pummel tyres into submission, opening the door to undercuts, safety cars, and mid-race position swings. A safety car is essentially guaranteed here — Norris won in 2024 precisely because of one. Managers who obsess over qualifying pace will get burned. Managers who back the fastest race-day driver will cash in.

---

## THE WEATHER

Miami in May means heat, humidity, and afternoon thunderstorms. Average highs sit around 30°C, tyre degradation will be aggressive, and the safety car probability — already high at this circuit — only climbs when the storms arrive. Rain during qualifying scrambles the grid. Rain during the race multiplies Fantasy points. Sprint weekend adds another session where the weather can cause chaos. This is not a weekend to load up on a single driver starting from pole.

---

## THE WEEKLY WATCHLIST

### 🟢 BUY

**Kimi Antonelli — $25.5m**
Championship leader. Two wins in three races. Miami's pole curse is irrelevant when you have the fastest car and the sharpest racecraft on the grid. Sprint weekend means extra scoring opportunities across the full weekend. If you don't own Antonelli already, Miami is not the weekend to keep waiting — the gap to the field is only growing.

**Oscar Piastri — $21.0m**
He won this race in 2025. He finished second in Japan. McLaren have genuine pace when the circuit suits them, and Miami's characteristics — multiple overtaking zones, high tyre deg — do suit them. At $21m he is the best pure value pick on the grid this weekend. Over $4m cheaper than Russell, comparable scoring ceiling. This is the pick.

---

### 🟡 HOLD

**George Russell — $28.0m**
He's consistent, he scores, he'll probably be on the podium. But at $28m you need wins, not fourths. Miami's safety car chaos can derail the most clinical of race plans, and Russell has a habit of being on the wrong end of timing windows. Hold him if you own him. Don't pay this price to bring him in now.

**Charles Leclerc — $23.5m**
Third in Japan, second in the constructors' battle, and genuinely in the championship conversation. But Ferrari's race pace in Florida heat has historically trailed Mercedes, and Leclerc has never won at Miami. His upside is real — his ceiling is a podium, maybe a win. But this circuit doesn't have his name on it yet. Hold and reassess post-Sprint Qualifying.

---

### 🔴 SELL

**Max Verstappen — $19.5m**
Red Bull have 16 constructors' points after three races. The RB21 is not competitive in 2026 — this is not a blip, it's the picture. Miami rewards raw package pace, and Red Bull simply don't have it. Verstappen's price still carries the weight of four world titles. That reputation is not scoring you Fantasy points this season. Sell now while the price premium holds.

**Lando Norris — $20.0m**
Yes, he won here in 2024 via a perfectly-timed safety car stop. That's not a repeatable thesis — that's circumstance. Japan was anonymous for Norris. McLaren are third in the constructors', 79 points behind Mercedes. His price reflects a narrative the car is no longer living up to. The Norris-Miami story is good content. It's bad Fantasy strategy.

---

## THE CHIPS

Miami is the first Sprint weekend of 2026, and chip selection matters. **No Negative** is your best play this weekend. Miami's safety car tendency, the Sprint DNF risk on a temporary street circuit with minimal pre-lock running, and the genuine storm threat all point to meaningful downside exposure. Sprint DNFs cost -10 in 2026 — not -20 like previous seasons — but on a circuit where you have one Free Practice before Sprint Qualifying locks your team, that protection is worth using. **Do not play Limitless.** One FP session before lock-in, McLaren and Ferrari both rumoured to bring upgrades, and a grid order that's genuinely unclear heading in. Limitless needs data. Miami doesn't give you enough of it.
