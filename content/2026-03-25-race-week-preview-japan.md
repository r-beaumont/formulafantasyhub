## Race Week Preview: Japanese Grand Prix

**Rob Beaumont** — Official F1 Fantasy columnist · formula1.com · 25 March 2026

*Mercedes have the championship in a stranglehold. The only question at Suzuka is whether you've got the right Silver Arrow.*

---

## THE SETUP

Two races in, Mercedes have this locked down — 98 points to Ferrari's 67, with Antonelli's maiden win in China tightening the internal battle. Russell leads by four points, but it's a coin flip between team-mates. Ferrari are the only genuine threat and they're 31 points adrift. Verstappen has 8 points. Red Bull are lost.

## THE CIRCUIT

Suzuka is a qualifying circuit. Full stop. Pole position here is worth more than anywhere else on the calendar — overtaking is borderline impossible without a major pace advantage. You need a 0.7–0.8 second performance gap just to make a move stick. Tyre degradation is low after last year's resurfacing, so pit strategy won't randomise results. The barriers are close and any contact tends to bring the safety car out. For Fantasy: pick the drivers who qualify well, not the ones hoping to recover from the midfield.

## THE WEATHER

The forecast is unsettled. Saturday qualifying is showing a 75% chance of rain, and race day carries 61% rain probability. If that holds, we're looking at potential chaos — safety cars, differential scoring, and real negative point risk. In wet conditions, No Negative isn't just a good chip. It's the right chip.

## THE WEEKLY WATCHLIST

### 🟢 BUY

**Kimi Antonelli — $23.2m**
He just won a Grand Prix. At $4.2m cheaper than Russell, Antonelli gives you near-identical expected points with far better PPM. Mercedes should dominate qualifying at Suzuka, and Antonelli is in the form of his life. This is the buy of the week — no debate.

**Oliver Bearman — $7.4m**
The best-value driver on the grid. PPM of 27.84, leading all drivers. Haas have been the standout midfield team through two rounds, and Bearman's consistent top-10 scoring gives your budget slots certainty. At that price, you can still fit two premiums elsewhere. Don't overthink it.

### 🟡 HOLD

**Charles Leclerc — $22.8m**
Leclerc is historically strong at Suzuka and Ferrari remain the only realistic threat to Mercedes. But at this price with Antonelli clearly the superior pick, he's a hold if you've got him — not a buy if you don't. Ferrari's pace gap to the Silver Arrows is still significant.

**George Russell — $27.4m**
Championship leader, should be in the mix at Suzuka. But at $27.4m the PPM maths don't work when Antonelli is doing the same job at $23.2m. If you own Russell, sit tight. If you don't, there's no reason to pay the premium right now.

### 🔴 SELL

**Max Verstappen — $27.7m**
The most expensive driver on the grid. Eight points to show for it. Red Bull are nowhere, and Suzuka used to be Verstappen's kingdom — that current car has no interest in revisiting those days. Get him out.

**Lando Norris — $27.2m**
Fifteen points at $27.2m. That is a catastrophic PPM number. McLaren hit trouble in China, and with overtaking near-impossible at Suzuka, starting position is everything. Until McLaren show they can qualify at the front consistently, Norris is nowhere near worth that price.

## THE CHIPS

If Saturday's rain forecast holds, play **No Negative**. Wet Suzuka means safety cars, chaos, and genuine negative point risk for anyone caught on the wrong strategy or the wrong side of a crash. The Bahrain and Saudi rounds have been cancelled — there is no double header coming. Miami is five weeks away. This is a perfectly clean week to use it. If you've already burned it, sit tight and hope for dry.
