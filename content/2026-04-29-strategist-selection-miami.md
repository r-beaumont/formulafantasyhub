## F1 FANTASY: Strategist Selection — What's the best line-up for the Miami Grand Prix?

**Rob Beaumont** — Official F1 Fantasy columnist · formula1.com · Wednesday, 29 April 2026

*Three races in. Two different winners. One team doing whatever it wants. Miami is the reset button you've been waiting for.*

---

### THE SITUATION

Mercedes have won all three races this season. Russell took Australia. Antonelli took China and Japan back-to-back. The Constructors' lead is already 45 points. Ferrari are keeping themselves in the conversation — Leclerc has been on the podium in two of three races, and Hamilton banked a surprise China podium that no one saw coming. McLaren showed teeth in Japan with Piastri's second place, but they're still chasing. Red Bull? Absent. Then Bahrain and Saudi were cancelled, leaving five weeks to fix your team before Miami. Do not waste it.

---

### THE CIRCUIT

Miami is a street-adjacent layout — tight walls, a long back straight into Turn 17, and a technical middle sector that punishes mistakes. Overtaking is possible but not easy; qualifying position matters. Tyre degradation is punishing in the May heat — 30°C+ track temperatures will push compounds hard from lap one, and a two-stop strategy is likely. Safety car probability is high. This is a Sprint weekend, which means two scoring sessions before Sunday's race: Sprint Qualifying Friday, the Sprint itself Saturday. Your captain pick matters more than usual.

---

### THE WEATHER

Hot, humid, and volatile. Miami in May means afternoon storm risk — forecasts show 84–86°F highs with a meaningful chance of thunderstorms on race day. If it rains, safety cars multiply, chaos follows, and No Negative becomes the best chip on the board. Keep an eye on the forecast as Wednesday approaches. If rain looks likely for Sunday, adjust your strategy accordingly.

---

### THE PICKS

---

**🔴 GEORGE RUSSELL — $27.4m ★ CAPTAIN**

He's the most consistent points scorer on the grid. Won Australia, backed up Antonelli in every race since. Mercedes are the fastest car by a distance, and Miami's power-sensitive layout suits them perfectly. Sprint weekends double his scoring exposure. Yes, he's your most expensive driver. He's worth every cent of it. This is your armband.

---

**🔴 KIMI ANTONELLI — $23.2m**

Two wins in a row. Youngest championship leader in history. At $23.2m he remains the best value in the premium tier — Russell costs $4m more for the same car. Antonelli in a Sprint weekend is a weapon. He qualifies well, he races cleanly, and he has momentum that doesn't stop at a price tag. Check his updated price in-game — it'll have moved up after Japan, but he's still a lock.

Podium in Australia. Fourth in China. Podium again in Japan. Leclerc is drip-feeding consistent returns and he loves a street-adjacent layout — this is exactly where he pulls a lap from nowhere in Sprint Qualifying. Ferrari's race pace is genuine. At $22.8m he's your most reliable mid-tier asset and the anchor of your budget.

---

### THE CONSTRUCTORS

**Mercedes** — this selection is non-negotiable. Two drivers, three wins, 45-point lead. They score from every session this weekend.

**Ferrari** — Leclerc and Hamilton have both podiumed this season. Ferrari are the second-best team in real life and in Fantasy right now. McLaren are the alternative if Piastri carries Japan's momentum — but Ferrari's consistency across three rounds wins the argument.

---

### THE CHIPS

Miami is one of the best chip windows of the 2026 season. It's a Sprint weekend, which means three scoring sessions (Sprint Quali, Sprint, Race) — your captain earns more, your constructors earn more, and every point is magnified.

**3X Boost**: Strong play here if the weather looks dry by midweek. Three scoring sessions = maximum multiplier value. If storm forecasts grow for Sunday, hold it — Silverstone is the alternative.

**No Negative**: If rain is confirmed for race day by Wednesday, flip from 3X Boost to No Negative. Miami chaos with no downside risk is a different kind of weapon entirely.

**Wildcard**: If you've been sitting on a squad you hate after the long break, this is the round to burn it. Five weeks of data should tell you exactly what's broken.

Play the chip that fits the forecast. Don't play both.
