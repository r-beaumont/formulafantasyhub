# 🏁 🇺🇸 F1 · Friday Debrief · 4 min read
## Friday Debrief: Miami Grand Prix
**Rob Beaumont — 1 May 2026**

*Kimi Antonelli ended Friday in Miami the way he's ended almost everything in 2026 — at the top of a timing screen that had no business belonging to a teenager in his sixth race weekend.*

---

### THE HEADLINE

Antonelli takes Sprint pole in Miami, beats Piastri and Norris, and the question is no longer whether he belongs — it's whether anyone can stop him.

---

### FP1 SNAPSHOT

This is a Sprint weekend — FP2 does not run, which made the extended 90-minute FP1 session the only data teams would get before Sprint Qualifying. **Oscar Piastri** used that time well, topping the timesheet at 1:27.128 on the soft compound and confirming McLaren's car is working at this circuit. **Charles Leclerc** slotted in second with Ferrari — noteworthy given the Scuderia arrived in Miami with a fresh upgrade package developed over the spring break — and **Max Verstappen** completed a top three that looked reasonable for Red Bull without setting any pulses racing. Williams had a quietly impressive session, with **Carlos Sainz** and **Alex Albon** both inside the top five. The two Mercedes — **George Russell** in P7, **Kimi Antonelli** in P9 — looked deliberately managed, running shorter stints and saving the real pace for when it mattered. A late red flag for an **Oliver Bearman** crash ended the session early and denied a handful of drivers a final run, but the picture was clear enough: McLaren and Ferrari showed their hands, Mercedes kept theirs hidden.

---

### SPRINT QUALIFYING SNAPSHOT

Then came Sprint Qualifying, and Mercedes showed exactly what was behind those playing cards. Antonelli produced a 1:26.482 in SQ3 that was simply in another postcode from the rest of the field — Piastri, who had looked fastest all day, wound up 0.045s behind in P2, with **Lando Norris** a further 0.055s back in P3. Verstappen qualified fourth, 0.255s off the pace — a gap that, on this circuit, will feel uncomfortable. **Russell** rounded out the top five, giving Mercedes a dominant one-three on the Sprint grid (Antonelli on pole, Russell starting fifth). Leclerc in P6 and **Lewis Hamilton** in P7 means Ferrari have both cars in points-paying Sprint positions, which is the best-case outcome after their update gamble. The session had a notable casualty: **Yuki Tsunoda** was eliminated in SQ1, the second time in three weekends Red Bull's second seat has flattered to deceive. **Alex Albon** in P8 and **Isack Hadjar** in P9 are the chart-toppers from the midfield pack. The Sprint grid is set — Antonelli leads, Piastri and Norris follow, and Verstappen will need a good start at Turn 1 or this Sprint could drift away from him quickly.

---

### FANTASY IMPACT

If you don't have Antonelli in your Fantasy team, today hurt. The 18-year-old is averaging 50 points per race weekend across a season that already features back-to-back Grand Prix victories in China and Japan, and Sprint weekends supercharge his ceiling — Sprint pole, fastest lap points, and podium bonuses all stack. He is not the cheapest option on the board but he is the clearest value at the top end right now. **Piastri** and **Norris** confirm McLaren remain a genuine threat — both in the top three on the Sprint grid — making the McLaren constructor a strong pairing with Mercedes if budget allows. The real value conversation, though, centres on Albon: P5 in FP1, P8 in Sprint Qualifying, consistent in a circuit that suits the Williams aero package. At his current price tier, Albon is the midfield name to have this weekend. On the downside, **Bearman**'s FP1 crash was not the preparation Haas needed, and Tsunoda's SQ1 exit makes him an easy drop if you're holding him. The **Limitless** chip managers sitting on it: Antonelli's ceiling in a Sprint weekend is significant.

---

### TOMORROW'S KEY QUESTION

Antonelli has the Sprint won on paper — but can Piastri and Norris manufacture a first-corner moment that reshuffles the order before Sunday's main qualifying, where the championship picture could shift dramatically?
