# F1 Fantasy 2026: Australian Grand Prix Picks & Preview

**Round 1 | Albert Park, Melbourne | March 6–8, 2026**

---

A new season. A new rulebook. And absolutely no idea who's going to be fast.

Welcome to the most exciting — and most terrifying — F1 Fantasy race of the year: the season opener. With sweeping 2026 regulation changes reshuffling the technical order, the usual playbook of "pick the dominant team" has been thrown out the window. This year, you have to read the pre-season tea leaves carefully. Fortunately, Bahrain pre-season testing gave us a few clues — and this guide will help you turn those clues into points.

---

## Albert Park at a Glance

Albert Park is a semi-street circuit that rewards precision over raw pace. At 5.278km per lap over 58 laps, it features a mix of fast flowing sections and tighter technical corners, with enough DRS zones to make overtaking viable. Historically, the circuit tends to favour cars with strong one-lap pace and tyre management, since track position matters enormously here.

Key F1 Fantasy implications for Melbourne:

- **Qualifying matters.** Grid position bonuses are easier to collect here than at power-unit tracks. Prioritise drivers with strong one-lap pace.
- **Safety car risk is real.** Albert Park's proximity to barriers means VSC or SC periods are common — great for mid-grid drivers that survive a shuffle and pick up points-finishes.
- **New rules = first-lap chaos potential.** With teams still learning the 2026 cars, reliability and race management are unknowns. Consider that when picking long-shot value plays.

## The 2026 Landscape: What Testing Told Us

Under the major 2026 regulation overhaul, the established order has been shaken up. Here's the condensed version for Fantasy purposes:

**Mercedes** looks like the team to beat. George Russell was consistently quick throughout Bahrain testing, and the W17 appears to have cracked the new hybrid energy deployment rules better than most. Russell is the +225 betting favourite for the race.

**Ferrari** were the fastest outright in testing. Leclerc set the quickest times across both test sessions in Bahrain, suggesting genuine one-lap pace. Their race pace and strategic flexibility also looked strong — they're arguably the most complete package heading into Round 1.

**Red Bull** are harder to read. Verstappen's raw talent keeps him in contention regardless, but the RB21 looked marginally less dominant under the new regs than we've been used to. Don't fade him — but he's not the slam-dunk he was in 2023 or 2024.

**McLaren** appear to have lost the clear pace advantage they held for much of 2025. Norris and Piastri are still quality picks, but value has shifted this season.

## Driver Picks

The budget is **$100 million** for five drivers and two constructors.

### Premium Tier

**George Russell — $27.4m | Mercedes**
Russell is the standout pick for Melbourne. He's the betting favourite, Mercedes looked strongest over a race distance in testing, and Albert Park suits a clean, precise driver. At $27.4m he's essentially the same price as Verstappen but arguably better value heading into this specific race. He's as close to a must-pick for Round 1 as it gets.

**Max Verstappen — $27.7m | Red Bull**
The most expensive driver on the grid, and still among the top picks. Verstappen's adaptability means he'll find performance even if the RB21 isn't quite at the sharp end — he rarely finishes outside the top five at any circuit. If you're going big in the premium tier, pair him with Russell.

**Charles Leclerc — $22.8m | Ferrari**
The best value in the top tier. Leclerc was the fastest driver in Bahrain testing, is brilliant in qualifying, and Albert Park rewards one-lap pace. At nearly $5m cheaper than Russell and Verstappen, he frees up significant budget for mid-tier picks. He's arguably the *highest* upside driver on the grid heading into this race.

### Mid Tier

**Kimi Antonelli — $23.2m | Mercedes**
A fascinating pick as the season-opener's wildcard premium. Antonelli takes over Russell's old seat and has the same machinery. In testing he showed genuine pace, and if Mercedes have the fastest car, he's a strong points scorer. Slightly riskier than the veterans, but the price reflects that — and the potential upside is huge.

**Isack Hadjar — $15.1m | Red Bull**
The best mid-tier pick on the grid. Hadjar steps into a Red Bull Racing seat this season and, if the car has any pace at all, he'll be collecting points consistently. At $15.1m he's a bargain compared to the established names and frees up significant budget below.

**Carlos Sainz — $11.8m | Williams**
Easy to overlook after his Ferrari exit, but Sainz arrives at Williams as a proven race winner who can extract the maximum from any package. Williams made genuine progress in 2025, and with Sainz now leading their charge, expect them to fight in the midfield. At $11.8m he punches well above his price bracket.

### Budget Tier

**Oliver Bearman — $7.4m | Haas**
One of the better budget picks of the grid. Bearman has shown he's not just a filler driver — his performances as a stand-in in 2024 and 2025 earned him this full-time seat. Haas have struggled for consistency, but Bearman will find points when opportunities arise.

**Franco Colapinto — $6.2m | Alpine**
At the minimum viable budget level, Colapinto is worth a look. He has the talent, he's shown race pace in flashes, and at $6.2m there's nothing to lose. In a chaotic opening race, budget picks near the back can quietly collect points from safety-car scrambles and reliability failures higher up the grid.

## Constructor Picks

Constructors offer more stable, predictable points returns than drivers — both cars contribute. For Australia, **Mercedes** is the top constructor pick given their strong testing performance. **Ferrari** are a close second and worth serious consideration given Leclerc's pace.

Avoid **Red Bull** as a constructor pick this round — the car looks a step off, and with Hadjar as the second driver, the ceiling on their combined points is lower than their rivals.

## Things to Watch This Weekend

**Will Mercedes' testing pace translate on race day?** This is the big question. Pre-season testing is notorious for teams sandbagging, and we won't know the true order until the lights go out on Sunday.

**How do the new rookie drivers handle the pressure?** Antonelli and Hadjar step into top-team seats under enormous expectations. Round 1 results will immediately tell us if we should be loading up on them or holding off.

**Reliability is the hidden wildcard.** First race of a major new regulations cycle, brand new power units, completely revised cars — DNFs are coming this weekend. That's bad news for premium picks but great news for patient mid-grid pickers.

## Final Verdict

In a normal opening race, you'd back the dominant team and move on. In 2026, the dominant team hasn't been confirmed yet — and that's what makes this round both terrifying and brilliant for Fantasy. The safest approach is to **split your premium picks across Mercedes and Ferrari** (Russell + Leclerc), load up on the best value mid-tier option available (Hadjar), and pad the rest of your team with the cheapest reliable options you can find.

Good luck in Melbourne. May your drivers finish, and may everyone else's hit the wall.
